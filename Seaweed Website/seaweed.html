Different Types of Seagrass Species

Seagrass Seagrass Code Description Image
Cymodocea Serrulata Cs The leaf of this plant features a serrated tip, a wide blade measuring 5-9mm in width, and typically ranges from 6 to 15cm in length, with 13-17 prominent longitudinal veins.
Halodule Univervis Hu Halodule Uninervis has trident leaf tip, 1 central vein and usually pale rhizome with clean black leaf scars.
Enhalus Acoroides Ea This plant is characterized by very long ribbon-like leaves with inrolled margins, a thick rhizome covered in long black bristles, and cord-like roots, with the leaves typically measuring 30-150 cm in length.
Thalassia Hemprichii Th Thalassia hemprichii has short black bars of tannin cells on leaf, thick rhizome with scars between shoots, "Sickle" shaped leaves that are 10-40 cm long.
Cymodocea Rotundata Cr Rounded leaf tip, narrow lead blade (2-4mm wide), Leaves 7-15 cm long, 9-15 longitudinal veins, well developed leaf sheath
Halodule Pinifolia Hp Rounded leaf tip, 1 central vein, usually pale rhizome, with clean black leaf scars
Halophila Decipiens Hd Small oval leaf blade 1-2.5cm long, 6.8 cross veins, leaf hairs on both sides
Halophila Ovalis Ho This plant has 12 or more cross veins and no hairs on leaf surface.
Halophila Minor Hm This plant has less than 12 pair of cross veins and small oval leaf blade.
Thalassodendron Ciliatum Tc This plant has a cluster of leaves on elongate shoot "sickle" shaped leaves with tip, ligule present, rhizome "woody".
Syringodium Isoetifolium Si This plant is cylindrical in cross section with leaf tip tapers to a point and leaves 7-30cm long.
